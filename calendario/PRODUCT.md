# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Brazilian FFXIV players in the Hall of the Novice EX [HoN] guild. They arrive from Discord or the main site to check upcoming learning parties, raids, alchemy sessions, and rituals. Their context is quick discovery — they want to know what's happening this week, what day a class is on, and whether they can attend. Admins (raid leaders, event organizers) manage the event calendar through a separate authenticated panel.

## Product Purpose

An interactive academic event calendar that serves as the guild's scheduling hub. Members browse events by day and month, filter by type (spells, tactics, alchemy, ritual), and see details at a glance. Admins create and manage events through a secure CRUD panel. Success means members never miss a learning opportunity and admins spend minimal time on scheduling logistics.

## Brand Personality

Scholarly, Warm, Immersive. Same voice as the main HoN site — a wise mentor who also happens to be your raid leader. The calendar feels like an ancient academy's schedule board: parchment textures, crystalline accents, and the quiet authority of a well-organized institution. Not a corporate calendar app, not a gaming event tracker — a living document of the academy's activities.

## Anti-references

Generic calendar apps (Google Calendar, Outlook) — sterile, utilitarian, no soul. Gaming event trackers with neon timers and countdown urgency. Corporate scheduling tools with flat design and no personality. The calendario should feel like it belongs in a fantasy academy, not a tech startup.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration**: Event information is the hero. Dates, times, types, and instructors must be instantly scannable. Ornament serves legibility, never competes with it.
2. **The parchment is the page**: The warm ivory foundation carries the same weight as the main site. Every surface, every card, every modal rests on that aged-paper warmth.
3. **Rhythm through type**: Event types (spells, tactics, alchemy, ritual) create visual rhythm through color and typography, not through cluttered icons or busy patterns.
4. **Admin as trusted tool**: The admin panel is functional and efficient, not flashy. It serves organizers who need to create events quickly, not browse a showcase.
5. **Mobile is the norm**: Many members check the calendar on phones between duties. The layout must work beautifully at every width, not just desktop.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard accessibility: readable text sizes, sufficient color contrast on the parchment palette, keyboard navigation for the admin panel, and screen-reader-friendly event listings. The calendar's color-coded event types should not rely solely on color — icons or labels must provide redundant cues.
